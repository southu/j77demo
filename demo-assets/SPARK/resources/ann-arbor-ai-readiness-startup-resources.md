## Responsible AI & Compliance: A Practical Checklist for Pilots and Procurement

Responsible AI isn’t a slogan—buyers often treat it as a **risk review**. Requirements vary by industry and buyer, but these steps are commonly useful for reducing friction.

### Responsible AI / model risk checklist (pilot-ready)
- **Data rights & consent:** Document what data you use and your right to use it.
- **Privacy basics:** Identify whether you handle personal, patient, or sensitive data; define retention and access controls.
- **Evaluation plan:** Define what “good” means (accuracy, false positives/negatives, latency, robustness) and how you’ll measure it.
- **Human-in-the-loop:** Clarify where humans review, override, or audit outputs.
- **Monitoring:** Specify how you’ll detect drift and performance degradation post-deployment.
- **Documentation:** Maintain a short “model facts” sheet (purpose, limits, training data summary, evaluation results).

### Procurement-ready additions (especially public sector and healthcare)
- Security questionnaire readiness (basic controls, incident response owner)
- Clear deployment model (cloud/on-prem/edge) and data flow diagram
- A plain-language summary of limitations and appropriate use

## Common Pitfalls in Ann Arbor (and How to Avoid Them)

- **Over-indexing on research without a buyer:** Balance technical credibility with weekly customer discovery.
- **Pilot stagnation:** Put a success metric and conversion step into the pilot agreement (what triggers paid expansion).
- **Underestimating procurement:** Start documentation early (security, privacy, evaluation).
- **Networking without an ask:** Every meeting should end with one clear request (intro, dataset, pilot sponsor, hiring referral).
