## Data, privacy, and regulatory: practical guidance for AI teams

If you’re building in health, mobility, security, or trust/safety, buyers will often ask about governance before they ask about features.

A practical starting checklist for AI founders:

- **Data provenance**: document where training and evaluation data came from, and the rights you have to use it
- **Security posture**: map your path toward enterprise expectations (often including SOC 2 planning)
- **Model risk and safety**: define how you test, monitor drift, and handle edge cases—especially in safety-critical environments
- **Procurement readiness**: prepare to explain your system’s limitations and failure modes

In Ann Arbor, the advantage is that many potential partners operate in regulated settings; founders who embrace that rigor early often move through enterprise diligence more smoothly.

## Common pitfalls for Ann Arbor AI startups (and how to avoid them)

1) **Underestimating enterprise cycle time** (especially automotive/mobility). Plan for longer pilots and procurement steps; define “pilot success” with the buyer upfront.

2) **Overbuilding before customer discovery**. Strong research culture can tempt teams to optimize models before validating the buying workflow.

3) **Treating compliance as a late-stage task**. In trust/safety, health, and mobility, credibility is part of the product.

4) **Hiring too narrowly**. AI teams often need a balanced early roster: product, data engineering, and customer-facing problem-solving—not only modeling.

Ann Arbor SPARK’s coaching and introductions can help founders pressure-test milestones, meet the right domain experts, and avoid spending cycles on the wrong “next step.”
