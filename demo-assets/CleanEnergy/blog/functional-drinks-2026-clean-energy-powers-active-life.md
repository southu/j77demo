## What “Functional” Should Mean (and What It Often Means Instead)

A functional drink can be a great tool when it matches a real need—energy, hydration, digestion support, or recovery.

In practice, “functional” often means one of two things:

1. **A targeted benefit** (energy, gut support, focus, recovery)
2. **A long list of added components** (isolated fibers, stimulant blends, vitamin megadoses, sweeteners, “natural flavors,” and more)

Clean Energy takes a food-first route. It’s functional because it’s built around **whole-food ingredients**—organic fruits and vegetables—rather than a stack of isolated add-ins.

When your schedule is full, your needs are usually straightforward:

- steady energy you can actually use
- hydration that feels comfortable
- nutrients that support training and recovery
- ingredients that don’t leave you feeling overstimulated

Clean Energy is designed to support a **natural energy boost** that many people describe as steadier and more “food-like” than a sudden jolt.

## How Clean Energy Compares to Trendy Functional Drinks

This isn’t about “good vs. bad.” It’s about choosing the right tool for your body, your goals, and your day.

Many modern functional beverages are built on familiar industry building blocks:

- natural caffeine sources (green tea, guarana, coffee)
- sweeteners (often stevia/monk fruit, sometimes sugar alcohols)
- functional add-ons (prebiotic fibers like inulin, amino acids, nootropics)

You can see examples of these common strategies on ingredient pages such as [GURU Beverage ingredients](https://guruenergy.com/pages/ingredients) and in industry discussions like [Transforming energy drinks with natural, organic ingredients](https://www.supplysidefbj.com/beverage-development/transforming-energy-drinks-with-natural-organic-ingredients).

Those approaches can be helpful for some people, but they may feel like an on/off switch—especially if you’re sensitive to stimulants or certain sweeteners.

Clean Energy’s difference is its **whole-food function**: organic fruits and vegetables as the foundation, so the benefits come from food-based nutrition.

## Addressing Common Questions (Before You Have to Ask)

### “Does it have sugar?”
Clean Energy gets its sweetness from **fruit-based ingredients**, which naturally contain sugars along with other nutrients. If you’re managing blood sugar, reducing added sugar, or following a specific nutrition plan, consider using Clean Energy **with a balanced snack** (protein/fat/fiber) and checking the label to make sure it fits your goals.

### “Is it a healthy energy drink without caffeine?”
Clean Energy is often used by people looking to limit stimulants. Because product formulas can vary, check the label for your specific Clean Energy product. Many people choose it specifically for a **more steady, food-forward energy** experience.

### “Will it bother my stomach?”
Everyone’s digestion is different. In general, whole-food ingredients can feel gentler than some heavily carbonated drinks or products packed with sugar alcohols or large doses of isolated fibers. If you have a sensitive stomach, start with a smaller amount and sip slowly.

### “How long does it last if it’s shelf-stable?”
Shelf-stable products vary by format and packaging. For the most accurate guidance, follow the best-by date and storage instructions on your specific Clean Energy product.

### “Does it fit low-carb or keto?”
Because Clean Energy is fruit- and vegetable-based, it may not fit strict keto. If you’re low-carb, you may still be able to work it in strategically (for example, around workouts), depending on your personal carb target.
